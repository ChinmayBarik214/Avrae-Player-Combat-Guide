Avrae Player Combat Guide

Joining Combat

To join combat, your DM must first start it. Once they have, proceed below to the following commands:

!i join [arguments]

This will add your active character to combat.

Common arguments:

  • -h
    hides AC/HP
  • adv/dis
    gives advantage/disadvantage on initiative roll
  • -p <#>
    places at prerolled init

Your Turn

It’s your turn! On your turn, the most common actions are either attacking or casting a spell:

Attacking

To attack, just use the same command you would use out of combat:

!attack <​attack name> -t <target name> [arguments]

Common arguments:

  • -t <target name>
    can be used in the case of attacks that target multiple creatures (such as a breath weapon).
  • -rr <num>
    how many attacks to make at each target.
  • hit
    The attack automatically hits.

To see a list of your character’s attacks, use !attack list

Casting a Spell

To cast a spell, it’s also the same command in and out of combat:

!cast <spell name> -t <target name> [arguments]

Common arguments:

  • -t <target name>
    can be used in the case of spells that target multiple creatures (such as Fireball).
  • -l <num>
    specifies the level to cast the spell at.
  • -i
    ignores Spellbook restrictions (e.g. when casting from an item, as a ritual, etc.)

To see a list of your spells, use !spellbook

Ending Your Turn

When you’re done with your turn, use this command to move to the next combatant:

!i next
HP Commands

These commands should help manually change the HP of a combatant.

To modify your character’s HP:

!g hp <value>

To set your character’s HP:

!g hp set <value>

To add temporary HP:

!g thp <value>
